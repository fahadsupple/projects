Strategy Notes

Why Location Pages — Plan 2 Rationale
Plan 1 builds topical authority (category pages). Plan 2 builds geographic authority (location pages). Together they signal to Google: Triple J is a Sydney furniture store with expertise across categories (Plan 1) and presence across the city (Plan 2). Location pages also reinforce GBP — each suburb page signals that area as part of Triple J's service territory, expanding map pack visibility without requiring additional physical stores.
18 suburbs · 5 Sydney regions · GBP + website compound effect
Root Keyword Selection — "furniture store [suburb]"
Tested three roots against GKP data across all Sydney suburbs:
  • furniture store [suburb] — 44 suburbs with volume, 90–1,600/mo range. Selected.
  • lounges [suburb] — 0 suburbs with any GKP volume. Rejected.
  • lounge furniture [suburb] — 2 suburbs total (sydney 40/mo, canterbury 10/mo). Rejected.
"Furniture store [suburb]" is the only viable root. SERP analysis confirms local search intent — exactly what location pages are designed for. Exception: Campbelltown uses "furniture campbelltown" (390/mo vs "furniture store campbelltown" at 110/mo — 3.5× advantage, same SERP).
General vs Location Split Decision
Plan 2 is 100% location pages — no general keywords. All uncovered general keywords were SERP-checked and rejected:
  • sofa bed sydney — Freedom #3, IKEA #4. Rejected.
  • dining table sydney — Amart #3, Temple & Webster #6. Rejected.
  • entertainment unit sydney — Freedom #3, Temple & Webster #6. Rejected.
  • outdoor lounge sydney — AMBER (specialist-dominated SERP) — held in Plan 1 Eligible Candidates for future evaluation.
Plan 1 already covers all clean general categories. Adding weak general keywords to Plan 2 would waste page slots that are better used on high-volume location targets.

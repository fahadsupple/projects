Strategy Notes

How Plan 1 and Plan 2 Work Together
Plan 1 builds category authority — 11 pages targeting lounge subcategories, bar stools, bedroom, and living room at the product level. Plan 2 builds geographic authority — the 4 general pages reinforce topical relevance while the 7 location pages signal service area and expand Google Business Profile map pack visibility across the Yagoona catchment. Category pages establish what Triple J sells; location pages establish where they serve. Both are needed for a complete local SEO footprint.
Plan 1 = category authority  ·  Plan 2 = geographic authority
General Pages — Selection Rationale
  • Sofas/Lounges hub — BDM priority category and highest margin. Hub page anchors the lounge entity on the domain. Combined 440/mo.
  • Leather Lounges — highest-margin validated lounge subcategory. Combined 460/mo. Clean SERP.
  • Bar Stools — 880/mo primary, +600% YoY growth. Highest GKP volume in the entire validated general pool. Only clean dining SERP across all dining categories tested (dining tables, dining chairs all returned reject-list nationals at positions 1–4).
  • Coffee Tables — 390/mo primary, clean secondary (marble coffee table sydney, 90/mo). Only living room accent SERP confirmed free of nationals at every position except Freedom #2 on primary (medium-term target). Secondary is the early-ranking entry point.
Location Pages — Catchment Area Logic
All 7 suburbs sit within 17km of 590 Hume Hwy, Yagoona. For a single-location physical furniture store, customers realistically travel 15–20km maximum. Ranking in suburbs beyond that radius generates impressions without conversions — a ranking with zero foot traffic has no business value. SW Sydney was selected because Yagoona sits in the Canterbury-Bankstown / SW Sydney corridor, with Liverpool, Parramatta, and Auburn all established furniture-buying catchments accessible via the Hume Hwy and M5. Suburbs beyond 20km (Alexandria, Paddington, Penrith, North Shore) were excluded regardless of volume or SERP quality.
All suburbs ≤17km from Yagoona showroom  ·  foot traffic is the conversion goal
Location Root — "furniture store [suburb]"
"Furniture store [suburb]" is the only viable location root. "Lounges [suburb]" returned 0 GKP volume across all Sydney suburbs tested. "Lounge furniture [suburb]" returned volume in only 2 suburbs. "Furniture store [suburb]" returned volume in 44 suburbs and is treated as a local search by Google — confirmed by map pack presence in SERPs for these terms. All 7 selected suburbs showed volume on this root.
GBP + Website — Mutual Reinforcement
Location pages and Google Business Profile reinforce each other. Location pages signal to Google that Yagoona is the base and SW Sydney suburbs are the service area — this expands the GBP map pack radius into those suburbs. GBP authority (reviews, citation signals) in turn reinforces organic rankings on the location pages. Both workstreams should run in parallel, not sequentially.

Strategy Notes

Domain Profile & Target Window
DR 8 — very weak domain. Currently ranking for 84 keywords with 211 estimated monthly traffic (Ahrefs). All keywords are Sydney-modified and within the ≤1,000/mo volume cap. Competition assessed via SERP — no Ahrefs KD used. Target: all pages ranking within 3 months.
Lounge Priority — BDM Brief Alignment
BDM brief flagged lounge furniture as client priority. 7 of 10 SEO pages target lounge subcategories. This builds topical authority across the lounge category faster than spreading keywords evenly — pages in the same category reinforce each other's rankings. All lounge SERPs confirmed low competition — dominated by smaller Sydney-local retailers, not national chains.
7/10 pages — lounge subcategories
Volume Cap & Competition Assessment
All keywords ≤1,000/mo. Homepage is the only exception (entity page — higher volume acceptable). Competition assessed by SERP composition — Ahrefs KD not used. Low competition = SERP dominated by smaller Sydney-local retailers (Sydney Furniture Factory, Monster Furniture, Known for Lounges etc.). If Freedom, Amart, Harvey Norman, IKEA, or Temple & Webster dominate a SERP (i.e. hold multiple top positions or control the top 3), that keyword was rejected. A single national chain appearing mid-page (position 6+) while local specialists hold the top positions does not disqualify a keyword — a competitive path into the local specialist cluster still exists.
Categories Excluded from Plan
  • Outdoor Furniture — all keywords either exceeded the 1,000/mo cap (outdoor furniture sydney 2,400/mo, outdoor sofa sydney 2,400/mo) or had discount/clearance-dominated SERPs ("for sale" variants returned Gumtree, clearance pages, and warehouse sales — wrong positioning for Triple J). Replaced with Corner Lounges.
  • Dining Room Furniture — dining chairs and dining tables return completely separate SERPs with different competitive sets. Mapping both to one page would split topical signal. Would require 2 additional pages not available in the 20-keyword budget.
  • Kitchen Furniture — SERP dominated by kitchen renovation and cabinet companies, not furniture retailers.
  • Lighting — SERP dominated by designer lighting specialists and electrical retailers. Wrong competitive set for a furniture store.
  • Home Office Furniture — SERP dominated by dedicated office furniture specialists (Equip Office Furniture, Elite Office Furniture, Northside Office Furniture). Triple J as a general retailer faces specialist-only competition in this SERP, not general furniture chains. Deprioritised in favour of the Coffee Tables page.
  • Bedroom pieces (individual) — storage beds, chest of drawers, bedside tables, tallboys, entertainment units, and buffets/sideboards all rejected. Every keyword at meaningful volume (>10/mo) returned at least one of: Amart, Harvey Norman, Temple & Webster, Freedom, Fantastic Furniture, IKEA. Coffee Tables selected as 10th page — the only living room/bedroom accent furniture SERP confirmed free of major national chains.
Pairing Rules Applied
Every pair validated against three rules before finalisation:
  • No subset pairs — if keyword A contains all words of keyword B, they cannot share a page
  • No same-keyword variants — keyword + location modifier (e.g. chaise lounge / chaise lounge sydney) treated as one keyword
  • SERP alignment mandatory — primary and secondary must return the same competitive set and page types on Google. All 10 pairs were SERP-checked before finalisation.
All 10 reporting pairs SERP-validated
Sydney Modifier Strategy
Sydney modifier applied to all local-intent keywords. Exceptions: "chaise lounge" and "recliner chairs" — no meaningful Sydney-volume variant exists in GKP, and both terms already return AU-based furniture retailers. National terms without city modifiers are appropriate for ecommerce category pages with Australia-wide delivery; Sydney modifier added where it exists in GKP to capture showroom-visit intent (Yagoona, Hume Hwy corridor).

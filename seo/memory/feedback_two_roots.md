---
name: When to use 2 root keywords for location pages
description: Two roots are justified when they capture meaningfully different searcher intents or lead quality, even if one root has near-zero suburb-level volume
type: feedback
---

A second root keyword for location pages can be justified even with 0 suburb-level GKP volume, if it meets the following criteria:

**Justified when:**
- The two roots capture genuinely different searcher intents (e.g., "conveyancer" = transaction processing vs "property lawyer" = legal advice)
- The second root targets higher-value leads (e.g., people who want a lawyer rather than a conveyancer represent higher-value enquiries)
- The second root reinforces a key brand differentiator (e.g., Accredited Property Law Specialist — lawyer, not just conveyancer)
- At least one suburb has confirmed volume for root 2 (proves the intent exists, even if others show 0)
- Competition is low across most suburbs (easy to rank = low cost to include)

**Not justified when:**
- Both roots mean essentially the same thing to searchers
- The client doesn't have a genuine differentiator to justify the positioning
- Root 2 competes on the same exact intent as root 1 (cannibalization risk)

**Why:** In australcontractors.com.au, a second root (core drilling) was rejected because suburb-level volume was zero AND there was no meaningful intent difference from the primary root. In wslegal.com.au, "property lawyer [suburb]" was kept because it captures a distinct, higher-value searcher intent and aligns with Trish's accredited specialist USP.

**How to apply:** When evaluating a second root, ask: (1) Is the searcher intent meaningfully different from root 1? (2) Does it reinforce a real brand differentiator? (3) Is competition low enough that 0-volume pages are still worth creating? If all three yes → include root 2.

**Note:** Each suburb still gets a separate page per root — never combine both roots on one page.

---
name: Melbourne targeting for local businesses — when it applies
description: For local businesses, Melbourne-level keywords only make sense for services that can be delivered remotely; proximity-dependent services should be covered by location pages instead
type: feedback
---

Melbourne-level general keywords are appropriate for some local business services but not others. The deciding factor is whether geographic proximity affects conversion.

**Services where Melbourne targeting makes sense (deliver remotely):**
- Wills, estate planning, probate, deceased estates — clients don't need to visit, documents handled digitally
- Business law, commercial leases — phone/email-based, clients travel or use online tools
- Any professional service where client location is irrelevant to service delivery

**Services where Melbourne targeting is NOT appropriate:**
- Conveyancing — buyers and sellers typically prefer someone local to the property area or their own suburb
- Trades — clients want someone nearby
- Any service where "near me" is a primary driver of conversion

**Why:** A Casey firm ranking for "conveyancer melbourne" may attract enquiries from Richmond or Carlton that don't convert — those clients will choose a local conveyancer. The same firm ranking for "conveyancer berwick" attracts clients in their actual catchment who are likely to engage.

**How to apply:** For each service a local business offers, ask: would a client from the other side of the city realistically hire this business? If yes → Melbourne keyword on a service page. If no → location pages only for that service, no Melbourne service page.

**In this project (wslegal):** Dropped conveyancer Melbourne service page entirely; covered conveyancing via 10 Casey suburb location pages. Kept Melbourne for wills, probate, business law, commercial lease.

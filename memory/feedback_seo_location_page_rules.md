## When to use 2 root keywords for location pages
A second root keyword for location pages can be justified even with 0 suburb-level GKP volume, if it meets the following criteria:

**Justified when:**
- The two roots capture genuinely different searcher intents (e.g., "conveyancer" = transaction processing vs "property lawyer" = legal advice)
- The second root targets higher-value leads
- The second root reinforces a key brand differentiator (e.g., Accredited Property Law Specialist)
- At least one suburb has confirmed volume for root 2 (proves the intent exists)
- Competition is low across most suburbs (easy to rank = low cost to include)

**Not justified when:**
- Both roots mean essentially the same thing to searchers
- The client doesn't have a genuine differentiator to justify the positioning
- Root 2 competes on the same exact intent as root 1 (cannibalization risk)

**Why:** In australcontractors.com.au, a second root (core drilling) was kept because it captures distinct intent. "Core drilling" was rejected for suburb level because suburb-level volume was zero AND there was no meaningful intent difference from the primary root at suburb scale. In wslegal.com.au, "property lawyer [suburb]" was kept because it captures a distinct, higher-value searcher intent and aligns with Trish's accredited specialist USP.

**Note:** Each suburb still gets a separate page per root — never combine both roots on one page.

## Home suburb maps to homepage, not location page
For local businesses, the business's home suburb/address should be incorporated into the homepage entity — not created as a standalone location page.

**Why:** A standalone location page for the home suburb is redundant — the homepage already signals that location through address, schema, and NAP consistency. A separate page competes with the homepage and wastes a location budget slot.

**How to apply:** When building location page sets, check if any suburb in the list matches the business's physical address. If yes, remove it from the location page roster, incorporate the location into the homepage strategy, and reallocate those freed budget slots to a new suburb or service page.

## City pages ≠ location pages — critical budget rule

**City-level pages are general keyword budget slots, not location budget slots.** Location keyword budget is exclusively for TRUE SUBURB-LEVEL pages only.

- Standard package: 10 general + 20 location (suburbs)
- If 2 extra general slots needed: 12 general + 16 location — because 1 extra general = 2 fewer location
- When tallying a plan, count every city page (e.g., /commercial-solar/sydney/) against the general budget
- **Regional cities (Gold Coast, Newcastle, Geelong, Sunshine Coast, Cairns etc.) also count as general — they are cities, not suburbs**
- Location keywords = actual suburb names only (e.g., Penrith, Dandenong, Ipswich — suburb-level geographic units)
- Do NOT count city or regional city pages as location keywords in the deliverable

**Why:** City pages and regional city pages target city-level commercial intent, which is a service/entity page, not a geographic micro-targeting page. Suburb pages are the true location layer.

## Suburb selection criteria
- Same LGA or within realistic travel distance
- Active property/commercial activity (check development, population, market activity)
- At least some competition to validate that people search for services there
- Exclude suburbs in different LGAs with their own established service communities — pages rank but don't convert
- **No directional suburb name modifiers:** Never use suburbs with directional words (North, South, East, West) — e.g., Cranbourne North, Hampton East. These create weak entity signals. Skip regardless of GKP volume.

## Always add Melbourne modifier for reporting clarity
Even when Melbourne-modified keyword has no GKP search volume, add the modifier.

**Why:** Location-modified keywords are much cleaner to present to clients. Avoids confusion about what geography the ranking applies to. Easier to rank for a modified term than an unmodified national term.

**How to apply:** If a secondary keyword has no Melbourne volume but the national term has volume and is otherwise justified, still append "melbourne" to the keyword. Note in the plan that the Melbourne modifier has no GKP volume but is included for ranking reporting clarity.

---

## Melbourne modifier exception rule (for secondaries only)
If a keyword has no Melbourne-specific search volume but the national/unmodified version has volume, the unmodified term CAN be used as a secondary keyword when:
- The service is genuinely offered by the client
- The CPC is high (indicates commercial value)
- There are no better Melbourne-modified alternatives available
- The term is NOT a subset of the primary keyword on the same page

**Why:** Melbourne modifier is preferred, but forcing it when there's no volume leaves the secondary slot empty or weak. A nationally-searched term still captures local searchers via Google's location detection. Unmodified secondary keywords also build topical entity signals.

**How to apply:** Only for secondary keywords, not primary. Always try Melbourne-modified version first. Use unmodified version as fallback only.

## Melbourne targeting for local businesses — when it applies
Melbourne-level general keywords are appropriate for some local business services but NOT others. Deciding factor: does geographic proximity affect conversion?

**Services where Melbourne targeting makes sense (deliver remotely):**
- Wills, estate planning, probate, deceased estates — documents handled digitally
- Business law, commercial leases — phone/email-based
- Any professional service where client location is irrelevant to service delivery

**Services where Melbourne targeting is NOT appropriate:**
- Conveyancing — buyers/sellers typically prefer someone local to the property area
- Trades — clients want someone nearby
- Any service where "near me" is a primary driver of conversion

**Why:** A Casey firm ranking for "conveyancer melbourne" may attract enquiries from Richmond or Carlton that don't convert — those clients will choose a local conveyancer.

**How to apply:** For each service a local business offers, ask: would a client from the other side of the city realistically hire this business? If yes → Melbourne keyword on a service page. If no → location pages only.

**Example (wslegal):** Dropped conveyancer Melbourne service page entirely; covered conveyancing via 10 Casey suburb location pages. Kept Melbourne for wills, probate, business law, commercial lease.

Always add the Melbourne (or relevant location) modifier to secondary keywords, even if the Melbourne-modified version has zero GKP search volume.

**Why:** When reporting rankings to clients, a location-modified keyword ("commercial lease solicitor melbourne") is much cleaner to present than an unmodified national term ("commercial lease solicitor"). It avoids confusion about what geography the ranking applies to, and it's easier to rank for a modified term than an unmodified one — so it also helps clients achieve visible results faster.

**How to apply:** If a secondary keyword has no Melbourne volume but the national term has volume and is otherwise justified (entity signal, high CPC, client offers the service), still append "melbourne" to the keyword. Note in the plan that the Melbourne modifier has no GKP volume but is included for ranking reporting clarity.

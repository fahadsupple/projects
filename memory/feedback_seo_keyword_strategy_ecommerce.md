## 2 keywords per page is not compulsory — pages can have 1, 2, 3, or more
A page can target 1 keyword only, or 3–4 keywords, as long as they all make sense and each would bring real business if ranked.

**Why:** "2 per page" is a planning convention, not a hard rule. Forcing 2 keywords can result in weak secondaries that add no value.

**How to apply:** If no clean secondary exists, run with primary only. If a 3rd or 4th keyword is closely related, clean SERP, and would generate business — add it. Do not add keywords just to fill slots. Do not exceed 4 keywords per page.

---

## Volume ceiling per keyword (non-homepage pages)
Keep all keywords at ≤ 1,000 monthly searches per keyword for non-homepage pages on weak domains (DR < 20).

**Why:** New/weak domains cannot realistically rank for high-volume terms in a 3-month window. Lower volume = lower competition = faster rankings.

**How to apply:** At keyword selection stage, filter out any keyword > 1,000/mo for all pages except homepage. Flag as long-term play if over threshold.

---

## SERP alignment is mandatory for every keyword pair
Before finalising any primary + secondary pair, check the actual Google SERP for BOTH keywords separately. Only map two keywords to the same page if they return the same competitive set and page types.

**Why:** Misaligned SERPs split the page's topical signal. A page targeting "dining chairs" and "dining tables" will win neither.

---

## Singular/plural are the same keyword — never pair them
GKP groups singular and plural forms. Never use these as primary + secondary — they contribute zero topical diversity.

---

## Avoid location+country modifier as keyword
Do not use keywords like "dining tables sydney australia" — the "australia" suffix is not a meaningful modifier.

**How to apply:** Strip "australia" from any city-modified keyword.

---

## Subset rule — strictly enforced
If keyword A contains all words of keyword B, then B is a subset of A. Never pair them on the same page. Test every pair before finalising.

---

## BDM priority category should dominate the keyword plan
When a BDM brief flags a specific category as client priority, allocate the majority of page slots to that category.

**Why:** More pages in a category builds topical authority faster.

---

## Subcategory strategy for ecommerce
Use subcategory pages (leather lounges, modular sofas, recliner chairs) rather than broad category pages where possible.

**Why:** A searcher for "leather lounges sydney" is closer to purchase than one searching "furniture sydney." Subcategory traffic converts better and is achievable faster on weak domains.

---

## No "cheap" keywords unless brand explicitly uses "cheap" positioning
Do not use "cheap [product] [city]" unless the client's website explicitly uses that language and prices are genuinely cheap.

---

## "Bedroom suite" + city modifier pulls hotel SERPs, not furniture SERPs
**Never use "[bedroom suite] [city]" as a furniture keyword.** "Bedroom suite sydney" returns Four Seasons, Langham, Park Hyatt — hotel accommodation entirely.

**How to apply:** At keyword selection, never pair "bedroom suite" with a city modifier.

---

## "For sale" + location modifier consistently pulls classified/clearance SERPs
Do not use "[product] for sale [city]" as a keyword target. Gumtree, Facebook Marketplace, and discount warehouse results dominate.

---

## Homepage entity-building strategy for competitive terms
When homepage target keywords are too competitive for a new/weak domain, place them on the homepage for entity-building only — not as reportable ranking targets.

**How to apply:** For DR < 20 domains with highly competitive homepage keywords (6,000+/mo, national chains dominating SERP): mark as entity-building only, exclude from client's reporting budget, allocate those keyword slots to a new page where quick rankings are achievable.

---

## National chain presence — threshold for rejection vs amber
- **Reject:** national chain (Amart, Harvey Norman, Temple & Webster, Freedom, Fantastic Furniture, IKEA) at positions 1–4
- **Amber (evaluate):** chain at positions 5–8 — check whether local specialists fill top spots
- Fantastic Furniture is NOT on the reject list; treat as a beatable mid-tier chain

---

## QA process — run SERP checks on ALL keyword pairs before finalising
Before any plan is considered final, run a live SERP check on every individual keyword in the plan. SERPs change.

**Why:** During v1.3 QA for triplejfurniture, 4 secondaries that had been previously validated were found to have national chains ranking when re-checked.

---

## "Kitchen furniture" → renovation/cabinet SERP, not furniture retailers
"Lighting" → designer specialist SERP, wrong competitive set. Always check what the SERP is actually returning before finalising any keyword.

---

## Size-specific sofa keywords (3 seater, 2 seater) dominated by national chains
Keywords like "3 seater sofa sydney", "2 seater sofa sydney" consistently return Freedom, IKEA, Lounge Lovers, King Living. Not suitable for DR < 20 domains.

**How to apply:** Skip size-specific sofa keywords for weak domains. Target product-type keywords instead (leather lounges, fabric lounges, chaise lounges).

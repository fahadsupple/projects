Key Findings & Executive Summary

⚠
86 URLs flagged non-compliant in spreadsheet — primarily injectable/filler terminology
The majority of non-compliant URLs use TGA-sensitive terms: anti-wrinkle-injections, dermal-fillers, lip-fillers, collagen-stimulating-injections, and brand/drug names (Aldara, Efudex). Under the Australian TGA Advertising Code and AHPRA guidelines, URLs containing names of Schedule 4 prescription medicines or that directly imply therapeutic outcomes via injection of a named substance constitute a form of advertising. Proposed replacements correctly shift to consultation-framed language.
ⓘ
46 proposed URLs flatten the site hierarchy — SEO risk to be considered
All 43 location-specific service pages (anti-wrinkle, dermal-filler, lip-filler suburbs) are proposed to move from /service-areas-near-you/[service]-[suburb]/ to /[service-consult]-[suburb]/ (root level). While TGA-compliant, this eliminates the parent /service-areas-near-you/ breadcrumb, fragmenting site architecture and potentially reducing topical authority signals for local SEO. Additionally, the HIFU page moves from /treatment-services/mint-thread-lifts/ to a standalone root-level path without a parent service section. A hierarchy-preserving alternative is suggested.
ⓘ
Content mismatch: /treatment-services/mint-thread-lifts/ultraformer-mpt-hifu/ proposed as /treatment-services/hifu-ultrasound-consultations/
The page content confirms this is about Ultraformer MPT (HIFU) only — not SkinTyte. However, the page title in the spreadsheet reads "SkinTyte Consultations | Infrared Skin Firming Advice" which is incorrect for this URL. The proposed slug is accurate for HIFU but the title tag needs correction. Recommended title: "Ultraformer MPT (HIFU) Consultations | Non-Surgical Skin Tightening | Shellharbour Skin".
ⓘ
Brand/drug names in URLs — Aldara and Efudex flagged for TGA review
/skin-cancer/skin-cancer-surgery/aldara/ uses a registered brand name (Imiquimod/Aldara) directly in the URL. Similarly, /treatment-services/medical-procedures/topical-skin-cancer-treatments/efudex/ uses the Efudex brand name. Under TGA Therapeutic Goods Advertising Code 2018, advertising prescription medicines by name to consumers is prohibited. The proposed replacements (/topical-management/ and /field-directed-therapy/) are appropriate. Note: /skin-cancer/skin-cancer-surgery/topical-skin-cancer-treatments-including-pdt-pdt-aldara-efudixcryotherapy/ is particularly problematic as it names multiple prescription products and contains typos.
ⓘ
Steroid injections URL — proposed change softens clinical terminology appropriately
/treatment-services/medical-procedures/steroid-injections/ — the page content includes therapeutic claims ("flatten and soften raised scars", "prevent scar from growing larger") which are concerning for TGA compliance. The proposed URL /keloid-and-scar-consults/ is better, though /treatment-services/medical-procedures/keloid-and-scar-consults/ would maintain hierarchy.
ⓘ
Mono threads URL inconsistency: /mint-thread-lifts-2/ vs /mint-thread-lifts/
Row 82 has /treatment-services/mint-thread-lifts-2/mono-threads/ while rows 83–85 use /treatment-services/mint-thread-lifts/ as the parent. The -2 suffix suggests a duplicate page or CMS error. The proposed URL (/treatment-services/thread-consultations/mono-threads/) resolves the inconsistency correctly.
⚠
What-is-an-aesthetic-registered-nurse: title proposes "Cosmetic Nurse" — contradicts page content
The page explicitly states practitioners should use only the title "Registered Nurse," NOT "Cosmetic Nurse" per 2025 AHPRA guidelines. Yet the proposed URL is /what-is-a-cosmetic-nurse/ and the title is "What is a Cosmetic Registered Nurse?" — this directly contradicts the page's own regulatory guidance. Recommended URL: /what-is-an-aesthetic-registered-nurse/ (current is fine, or alternatively /aesthetic-registered-nurse-role/). The title should be updated to "What is an Aesthetic Registered Nurse? | AHPRA Guidelines | Shellharbour Skin".
✓
No duplicate URLs found across all 239 entries
Both current and proposed URL sets are unique — no exact duplicates detected in either column.
✓
153 compliant URLs follow strong SEO and TGA principles
The compliant section (skin cancer, laser, facials, medical procedures, blog posts, team pages) uses consistent hyphenated slugs, correct hierarchy depth, descriptive but non-therapeutic language, and appropriate breadcrumb structure. The /treatment-services/ parent hierarchy is well-maintained for non-injectable services.

Regulatory context: The Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) Advertising Code 2018 (s.4, s.22) prohibits advertising prescription medicines (Schedule 4) to Australian consumers. AHPRA's Guidelines for Registered Health Practitioners (updated 2025) require that all advertising by or about health practitioners complies with the TGA advertising framework. URLs form part of the digital advertising presentation. Named prescription injectables (botulinum toxin preparations, hyaluronic acid fillers when listed as S4), brand names, and explicit outcome claims in URLs constitute advertising within the meaning of s.3 of the Therapeutic Goods Act 1989. Note: The TGA takes a broad view of "advertising" that includes any material that promotes use of a therapeutic good to consumers — including URL slugs that appear in Google search results.

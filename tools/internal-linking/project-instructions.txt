CORE INTERNAL LINKING RULES
-----------------------------

QUANTITY
  - 2–5 contextual links per 1,000 words; 4–5 links per article is the sweet spot
  - Maximum 7 links for 3,000+ word pieces; keep total page links under 150

ANCHOR TEXT
  - Descriptive, keyword-rich, 2–5 words
  - Mix types: exact-match, partial-match, branded, compound, semantic
  - NEVER: "click here", "learn more", "this article", or vague anchors
  - NEVER: same anchor text pointing to different pages (cannibalization)
  - Warn at 40% concentration in any single anchor category (LOOM rule)

PLACEMENT
  - Body paragraphs = primary placement zone
  - First 1/3 of article: 1.5x priority weight; last 1/3: 0.5x weight
  - NEVER place inside H1–H6, inside existing <a> tags, inside code blocks, intro paragraphs (except for pillar links)

TOPIC CLUSTER / HUB-AND-SPOKE
  - Pillar pages link DOWN to all cluster articles
  - Cluster articles link UP to their parent pillar (mandatory "one link up" rule)
  - Keep ALL important pages within 3 clicks of homepage

MONEY PAGES
  - Money pages should RECEIVE many internal links but NOT leak equity to non-conversion pages
  - Base boost: +0.50 scoring; deficit boost: +0.25; leakage penalty: -0.30

ORPHAN PAGES
  - Every page must have at least one incoming internal link
  - Orphan pages get bonus priority for receiving new links

CONTENT QUALITY (CRITICAL)
  - Any inserted text must read as if it was part of the original — not bolted on
  - Read the FULL paragraph and surrounding paragraphs before writing any insertion
  - Match the site's tone, writing style, sentence patterns, and AU English
  - If no natural insertion point exists, do NOT force it — find a different paragraph

PROHIBITED
  - Forced/unnatural link placement
  - Excessive links in a single paragraph
  - Identical anchor text on multiple pages pointing to different targets
  - Links that interrupt reading flow
  - Linking to irrelevant content

LOCATION KEYWORD ANCHOR TEXT — CRITICAL RULES
----------------------------------------------

Before writing any anchor text for a location keyword:
1. Find company's actual physical location (About, Contact, footer)
2. Find their stated service area (Areas/Locations page)

SAFE anchor text patterns:
  "electrical services in Blaxland", "servicing Blaxland", "Blaxland" (neutral),
  "electrician near Blaxland", "serving Blaxland and surrounding areas"

UNSAFE anchor text patterns — AVOID:
  "our Blaxland electricians" (implies staff stationed there)
  "local electrician in Blaxland" (implies locally based)
  "electrician team in Blaxland" (implies team is based there)

Surrounding sentence must use: "We service...", "including...", "across...", "covering..."
NOT: "Our team in...", "Our office in..."

Always reference the broader service region alongside the suburb.

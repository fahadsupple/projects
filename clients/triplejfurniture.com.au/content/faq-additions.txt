A: Australian hardwoods such as Blackbutt, Spotted Gum and Tasmanian Oak perform exceptionally well in Sydney's humid, coastal climate when properly seasoned and finished. Q4: How do I care for timber furniture in Sydney's humid climate?
A: Regular cleaning with a dry or slightly damp cloth removes surface dust, while a quality timber wax or oil applied once or twice a year keeps the finish nourished and protected. Avoid placing pieces in direct strong sunlight or near air-conditioning vents, as rapid temperature changes can cause movement. Our team can advise on care products suited to the specific timbers in your pieces.

Q5: What's the difference between solid timber and veneer furniture?
A: Solid timber furniture is built from thick planks of real wood throughout, making it refinishable, repairable and genuinely long-lived. Veneer furniture uses a thin layer of real wood bonded to a substrate — it can look similar initially but can't be sanded or refinished if it chips or wears. For Sydney homes and families, solid timber is the long-term investment choice.

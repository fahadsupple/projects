1. Entity Architecture Being Built

Tier 1 — Homepage: Criminal Lawyer Melbourne (Umbrella Entity)

The homepage targets criminal lawyer melbourne as primary — the highest-volume umbrella term in the practice. This signals to Google: Anaki Lawyers is a Melbourne criminal law firm. The secondary keyword criminal defence lawyer melbourne (non-subset — "criminal lawyer" vs "criminal defence lawyer" differ meaningfully) anchors the specialist positioning — this firm does not do conveyancing or family law, it does defence. The homepage entity is reinforced by the address (263 William Street), the practicing certificate schema (Supreme Court VIC + High Court AU), and the 24/7 hotline as a differentiating trust signal. After indexing, Google understands: Melbourne CBD criminal law firm, specialist in defence, direct access to principal lawyer.

Tier 2 — Service Pages: Topical Authority Across All Major Offence Categories

The 10 service pages collectively build topical authority across the full spectrum of criminal and traffic law in Victoria. Each page is a dedicated entity for a specific offence type. Together they signal to Google that this is a firm with expertise across: drink driving, drug offences, assault, bail, sexual offences, intervention orders, traffic law, licence issues, theft, and fraud. This is critical for a new site — topical breadth accelerates entity recognition and surfaces the site for a wider range of queries beyond just the primary targets. The drink driving page (primary: drink driving lawyer melbourne, ~500–800/mo) is the highest-volume individual service page and a fast-ROI target. Intervention orders is deliberately included as a separate page — the IVO search cluster is substantial and distinct enough from criminal defence to justify its own entity.

Tier 3 — Location Pages: Geographic Presence Near Major Magistrates Courts

Six location pages are built around Victoria's busiest Magistrates Courts: Dandenong, Ringwood, Sunshine, Broadmeadows, Frankston, and Werribee. The rationale is specific to criminal law: criminal matters are tried at the Magistrates Court closest to where the alleged offence occurred or where the accused resides. Someone charged in Dandenong searches for a "criminal lawyer dandenong" because they're appearing at Dandenong Magistrates Court. This firm's state-wide coverage means these pages are genuine — not fabricated geographic signals. The single root "criminal lawyer [suburb]" is used across all location pages (no second root) because at suburb level the searcher intent is identical regardless of whether "criminal lawyer" or "criminal defence lawyer" is used — the SERP is the same. The homepage suburb (Melbourne CBD) is NOT given a standalone location page — it is anchored to the homepage via address, schema, and content, consistent with the home suburb rule.

The Overall Picture

After full execution, Google will understand Anaki Lawyers as: a Melbourne CBD-based criminal defence and traffic law specialist (not a generalist), personally run by Aabed Anaki (a named entity with qualifications and practicing certificates), with genuine geographic presence covering Melbourne's major court catchments, offering criminal defence representation across all major offence categories under Victorian law. This is a tightly-scoped entity with clear topical authority in one vertical — which is SEO-optimal for a new site competing against established generalist firms.

3. Beyond This Keyword Plan — What Else Is Needed

Google Business Profile (Critical — Do First)

For a criminal law firm, Google Business Profile is the single most important asset in the first 12 months. People arrested or charged at night search on their phone, see the local 3-pack, and call. The GBP must:

  • Primary category: Criminal Justice Attorney (or Criminal Defense Attorney — verify in GBP options)
  • Secondary categories: Traffic Law Attorney, Family Law Attorney (for IVO coverage)
  • Service area: set to cover all of Victoria (state-wide as per client's brief)
  • Address verified: Level 19, 263 William Street, Melbourne VIC 3000
  • Hours: 9am–5pm Mon–Fri with "Open 24/7 for urgent matters" noted in description
  • Services: list all major offence categories individually in the GBP services section
  • GBP description: mention 3-hour response guarantee, free 30-min consultation, Legal Aid, payment plans
  • Post regularly: case outcomes (non-identifying), legal updates, court process explanations
  • Review strategy: after each successful matter, prompt client to leave a Google review. Even 5–10 reviews can significantly move local pack rankings for a new firm.

"Near me" keywords (criminal lawyer near me, traffic lawyer near me) are won through GBP, not through service pages. The /areas-we-serve/ page reinforces GBP but does not replace it.

Schema Markup

  • Homepage: LegalService schema nested within LocalBusiness — specify areaServed as Victoria, legalServices covering all major practice areas
  • About page: Person schema for Aabed Anaki — name, jobTitle (Criminal Defence Lawyer), alumniOf (law school if available), hasCredential (Supreme Court VIC practicing certificate)

  • Location pages: LocalBusiness with areaServed set to the specific suburb
  • FAQ schema: On every service page — mark up the FAQ sections that answer "what happens if I'm charged with X" type questions. High PAA (People Also Ask) capture potential for criminal law queries.

NAP Consistency

  • Exact name, address, phone must match across: website, GBP, all directory listings
  • Use: Anaki Lawyers | Level 19, 263 William Street, Melbourne VIC 3000 | 0481791185
  • Key legal directories: LawAdvisor, Avvo, Justia (AU), Lawpath directory, Yellow Pages, TrueLocal, Yelp
  • Critical: Victorian Legal Services Board register — ensure the firm listing there matches GBP/website
  • Law Institute of Victoria (LIV) directory listing — if membership is available

Internal Linking Structure

  • Homepage → all service pages (give weight to Tier A pages first)
  • All service pages → homepage (establish parent entity relationship)
  • Service pages → related service pages (e.g., assault page → bail applications page — logical user flow after an assault charge)
  • Location pages → homepage + nearest service pages
  • /areas-we-serve/ → all location pages (hub-and-spoke)
  • Drink driving page → traffic lawyer page → licence suspension page (natural escalation flow)

Content Production

Every service page needs original, non-templated content built around the supporting entities listed in Section 2. Do NOT use the same template across all location pages — Google's Helpful Content system specifically flags thin, templated location pages. Each location page should contain court-specific information (court name, address, types of matters) that is unique to that location.

Priority content production order: Homepage → Drink Driving → Drug Offences → Assault → Intervention Orders → Bail → Traffic → remaining service pages → location pages.

Review Strategy

Criminal law reviews are challenging — clients are reluctant to leave public reviews about criminal matters. Strategy: (1) Prompt clients specifically at the close of a successful matter with a private link to the GBP review form. (2) Focus on professionalism and communication themes (the firm's key differentiators) rather than case outcome. (3) Target 10 reviews in year 1 — even this number can establish significant map pack visibility for a new firm.

Blog / Content Quick Wins

A content blog is not recommended in year 1 for a new site with limited resources. Instead, prioritise getting all service pages live with high-quality content. Once the service pages are live and beginning to rank, FAQ content embedded in service pages (marked up with FAQ schema) will surface in PAA boxes and provide informational traffic without requiring a separate blog strategy.

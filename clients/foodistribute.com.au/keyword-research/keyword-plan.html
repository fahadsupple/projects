Keyword Strategy

Strategy Rationale: 50 General + 4 Location

Why this is a national strategy, not a local-first strategy

Foodistribute ships nationally. Its customer base spans cafes in Sydney, restaurants in Brisbane, caterers in Adelaide, and B2C buyers across all states. Unlike a local plumber or lawyer, it does not lose relevance by ranking nationally — every ranking brings potential customers regardless of city.

However, the data shows that volume concentrates at the city level for distribution terms: "wholesale food Sydney" (140/mo), "wholesale food Melbourne" (140/mo), "food distributors Melbourne" (880/mo). The 4 location pages capture these city-specific buyers. The 50 general pages build national authority on what Foodistribute does and sells.

50 service/category pages + 4 city pages = cleanest architecture for a national distributor
Why product category pages matter despite zero-volume keywords

Most category-level wholesale terms ("wholesale cheese supplier australia", "wholesale chocolate supplier australia") return zero in DataForSEO — but Foodistribute's existing rankings prove these pages attract real traffic. The site ranks #3 for "wholesale bakery supplier" (170/mo) on a page that already exists. It ranks #5 for "callebaut chocolate sydney" (170/mo) on its Chocolate category page. Pages built and optimised for product categories are already earning traffic — the gap is that 8 of 11 categories have no dedicated, optimised page.

Zero-volume at national level also does not mean zero volume at long-tail. A buyer searching "wholesale callebaut chocolate 5kg australia" will land on a well-structured category page. These are high-conversion, low-competition landings.

Category pages = compound long-tail value, not volume plays
How the 50 general keywords are allocated

Tier A — Core distribution/wholesale identity (14 keywords): Homepage + 6 B2B service pages covering the core distributor + cafe/restaurant/bakery supplier intent. These target the highest-volume, highest-CPC terms.

Tier B — Product category pages (22 keywords): One page per product category (11 categories × 2 keywords each = primary + secondary). These build topical authority and capture long-tail category searches.

Tier C — B2C + specialty intent (8 keywords): Pages targeting the B2C buyer segment (specialty food delivery, gourmet food online) and the cross-cutting "wholesale food suppliers to the public" intent.

Supporting pages (6 keywords): Bakery suppliers, snacks/crackers, catering food supplier — cross-category pages that address buyer segments not covered by pure category pages.

Quick wins identified (existing rankings to optimise)

DataForSEO ranked keywords analysis shows Foodistribute at positions 4–6 for several high-value terms. These pages exist but are under-optimised. The plan maps primary keywords to existing pages where they align:

Competitive Gap Summary

The market is split into two tiers: (1) large national broadline operators — Bidfood, PFD Foods, Campbells, Metcash — who serve institutional accounts with bulk commodity products; and (2) regional/specialty operators — Moco, Complete Food Services, Galipo, Eustralis — who are city-specific and lack national reach.

Foodistribute sits in neither camp. It has national reach (like tier 1) combined with specialty products, B2C access, and premium brands (like a specialty tier 2 player). No competitor occupies this exact position. The keyword strategy builds pages that own this niche rather than fighting head-to-head with Bidfood or PFD on commodity broadline terms.

Foodistribute's gap: own "specialty wholesale" nationally + city pages where nationals are weak on specialty
Foodistribute Domain Authority Context

Foodistribute: Rank 177, 221 referring domains, 392 backlinks. Currently ranks for 4,562 keywords nationally — demonstrating strong crawl coverage already.

The domain is competitive with mid-tier regional operators (Complete Food Services: rank 63, Galipo: rank 146) but below the national majors (PFD: rank 186, Bidfood: rank 258). KD targets in this plan are calibrated to terms where rank 177 is competitive — mostly KD 0–26 range.

KD ceiling: target KD 35 or below for 90-day wins; KD 36–50 for 6-month targets; KD 50+ only as strategic 12-month bets

The 50 general keyword budget is allocated as follows: 6 core B2B service pages (homepage + 5 buyer-segment pages) · 11 product category pages (one per product category) · 2 B2C specialty pages · 2 cross-cutting service pages · 4 city-level general pages (Sydney/Melbourne/Brisbane/Adelaide — distinct from the 4 location pages which target "wholesale food supplier [city]") · 1 supplementary Brisbane page · remaining slots held for secondary keywords on existing pages.

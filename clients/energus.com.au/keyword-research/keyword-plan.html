Entity Structure Being Built
This keyword plan constructs a three-tier entity architecture that teaches Google what Energus is, what it does, and where it operates across Australia.
  • Tier 1 — Homepage (National Brand Entity): The homepage entity signals: "Energus is Australia's leading commercial solar and battery storage installer — a Top 5 C&I Retailer with ISO certifications, 55MW+ delivered, operating nationally." The homepage targets national commercial solar terms and brand-level identity. No city modifier on the homepage — Energus operates in all states.
  • Tier 2 — Service Pages (Topic Entities): Each service page builds a distinct topic cluster. Commercial Solar Hub (/commercial-solar/) is the parent hub linking to all sub-pages. Individual pages for installation, panels, installer, cost, industrial solar, BESS, solar for business, finance, eARC, and project management each establish Energus as authoritative in that specific topic. Together, these pages tell Google: "Energus is not a generalist — this business has deep expertise in commercial C&I solar and battery storage."
  • Tier 3 — Location Pages (Geographic Entities): The 8 city pages (4 cities × 2 silos) build geographic entity associations at capital-city level. Commercial solar city pages (/commercial-solar/sydney/, /melbourne/, /brisbane/, /adelaide/) associate Energus's commercial solar identity with each state market. BESS city pages (/battery-storage/sydney/ etc.) associate the battery storage identity with each market. City-specific electrical contractor licences (NSW, QLD, SA) are the credential proof on each location page.
  • The overall picture Google builds: Homepage (national C&I solar brand) → Service pages (topic authority across commercial solar + BESS + specialty services) → Location pages (geographic authority in NSW, VIC, QLD, SA). The full entity structure positions Energus as Australia's credentialled commercial solar and battery storage specialist.

Why Two Service Silos (Commercial Solar + BESS)
The two service silos are not marketing segments — they are distinct search intent clusters that require separate page architectures.
  • Silo 1 — Commercial Solar: Higher search volumes (880/mo for "commercial solar panels"), cleaner buyer intent, direct path to quote. The competitive SERP is mid-tier specialists (Smart Commercial Energy, Tindo Solar, HD Commercial) — none of which carry Energus's Top 5 C&I credential or ISO certifications. Energus's primary revenue driver.
  • Silo 2 — BESS / Battery Storage: Lower volumes at city level (0–10/mo) but transformative deal sizes ($100k–$2M+ per installation). The BESS SERP is structurally different from commercial solar — exact-match domain players (bessaustralia.com.au) and government agencies (ARENA) dominate national terms, but city-level BESS pages are entirely unclaimed. Energus enters this space first at city level, positioning for the volume growth that will come as BESS adoption accelerates.
  • Why separate location pages per silo: A Sydney buyer searching "commercial solar sydney" has different needs from one searching "battery energy storage system sydney." Separate pages allow each to rank independently for its search cluster and provide tailored content — solar ROI vs BESS resilience/cost-control positioning.

Beyond This Keyword Plan — What Else Is Needed
The keyword plan defines which pages to create and what to optimise them for. The following elements are required outside this plan's scope to make the entity structure perform:
  • Google Business Profile (GBP) — multi-location setup: Energus needs a GBP listing for North Sydney (primary). For Melbourne, Brisbane, and Adelaide city pages to rank in local pack results, Energus ideally needs verified GBP listings in each city (either a registered office address or a confirmed service area). At minimum: primary GBP in North Sydney with service areas set to all 4 states. Category: "Solar Energy Contractor" + "Electrical Contractor". "Commercial solar near me" and "BESS installer near me" queries are won through GBP, not service pages.
  • Technical SEO — URL fixes (urgent):
    • /industrial_solar_energy/ → /industrial-solar/: Underscores in URLs are a technical SEO issue — create new hyphenated URL and 301-redirect the underscore version.
    • Battery page consolidation: Three battery-adjacent pages exist (/battery/, /battery-for-business/, /commercial-battery-installer/) — clear cannibalisation risk. Consolidate to /battery-storage/ as the canonical URL and 301-redirect all three originals.
    • /locations/nsw/ archive page: Must be noindexed before building /commercial-solar/sydney/ to prevent cannibalisation of the city page.
  • Schema markup: LocalBusiness (type: SolarEnergyContractor) on homepage. Service schema on all service pages. FAQPage schema on BESS and cost pages (common buyer questions). BreadcrumbList on all pages. AggregateRating schema if Energus has Google reviews. Organization schema with all state licences listed. City pages should include areaServed (state name + city name) in schema.
  • NAP consistency across states: The North Sydney headquarters address must be consistent across the website, GBP, CEC member directory, Master Electricians directory, and all state business directories. Where Energus has a physical presence in other states (SA, QLD), those addresses should be verified and listed consistently. Inconsistent NAP weakens geographic entity signals at city level.
  • Internal linking structure: Homepage → Commercial Solar Hub → all service pages. Battery Storage page → BESS city pages. Each city page → link back to its parent service page (commercial solar city pages → /commercial-solar/, BESS city pages → /battery-storage/). The /solar-project-management/ page → link to commercial solar installer and industrial solar pages. Strong internal linking passes entity authority from the hub pages down to city and specialty pages.
  • Content production — quality over volume: The commercial solar pages (panels, installer, installation, hub) will rank against mid-tier competitors with modest content quality — Energus can outrank them with well-structured 1,000–1,500 word pages backed by real project data (55MW delivered, case studies, 1,800+ quotes). BESS pages need technical depth (C&I battery applications, AS/NZS 5139 compliance, ARENA funding context) that government sites and generic directories cannot provide. Location pages must reference state licences, state-specific incentives, and local case studies — not template content.
  • Case study content: The Sydney Art Gallery Extension (300kW, with Richard Crookes) is a named, verifiable project with a prestigious client. This is the highest-value E-E-A-T signal in Energus's arsenal. A dedicated case study page or detailed project section linked from the solar project management page directly addresses the construction company buyer's due diligence process.
  • CEC & directory listings: Clean Energy Council installer directory listing, Master Electricians Australia member directory, New Energy Tech Approved Seller directory, SolarQuotes listing, SolarReviews listing. These provide domain authority (citations), buyer discovery through comparison sites, and reinforce the entity's legitimacy signals to Google.
  • Review strategy: Google reviews directly impact local pack rankings. Energus's commercial clients (CFOs, project managers) are less likely to leave reviews spontaneously than residential customers — a structured ask at project commissioning (handing over documentation) is necessary. Even 20–30 reviews at 4.5+ stars strengthens local pack visibility in all 4 cities.
  • Blog / quick-win content: Not included in this keyword plan scope, but high-CPC informational terms ("commercial solar ROI", "BESS vs solar", "commercial solar rebates australia", "Section 40 depreciation solar") are worth targeting via blog posts to capture evaluation-stage buyers. These should be linked to the relevant service pages to pass authority signals.
Note: The keyword plan alone will not drive leads. Technical URL fixes (underscores, battery page consolidation, archive noindex) are urgent prerequisites — these must be actioned before or alongside the page builds.
Keywords Excluded and Why
  • Solar battery storage (1,600/mo): Mixed residential/commercial SERP — residential battery storage dominates. No commercial intent qualifier. Excluded per B2B-only rule. Covered as a supporting entity term on /battery-storage/ page.
  • BESS (8,100/mo national): Extremely high volume but the SERP at the bare unmodified term is dominated by Wikipedia, government agencies, utility companies, and industry publications — informational intent, not commercial buyer intent. Replaced with "battery energy storage system australia" (1,000/mo) which maintains the commercial qualifier and has a more winnable SERP.
  • Solar panel installation / solar installation (high volume, national): Residential intent contamination — SERPs for these national terms show residential solar companies and comparison sites (SolarQuotes, SolarChoice). Not commercial B2B intent. Excluded per B2B-only rule.
  • Solar monitoring / solar servicing / energy consulting: Insufficient search volume for standalone pages. Covered as supporting entities and content sections on existing service pages (BESS page, solar for business page). These services are won through existing client relationships, not organic search.
  • Rooftop solar commercial: SERP shows mixed intent including residential. Covered as a supporting entity on the commercial solar panels page.
  • Commercial solar cost per kw (0/mo): Zero GKP volume. Covered as a section within /commercial-solar-cost/ content — the cost page will naturally rank for cost-per-kw queries from the content depth.
  • Procurement and logistics / installer training: Near-zero search volume. B2B services sold through relationships, not organic search. Excluded from keyword plan — not even covered as secondary terms (no search demand confirmed).
  • Solar for property groups / solar for developers: Insufficient GKP volume as a standalone keyword cluster. Property developer buyers are captured by the solar project management page (/solar-project-management/) and commercial solar installation page (/commercial-solar-installation/) — these serve the new construction buyer intent where property developer searches land.
  • State/city combinations beyond the 4 licensed states (e.g., solar installer perth, commercial solar wa): Energus does not hold a WA electrical contractor licence — not included. Western Australia excluded from all location pages.
  • AGL / Todae Solar / TSP Energy branded terms: Competitor branded terms are not included in organic keyword plans — they belong in paid search campaigns.

Key Findings from Research
  • Commercial solar is a low-volume, high-CPC niche. Top keywords average $30–$40 CPC — a strong signal that searchers are buyers, not browsers. Low volume does not mean low value here.
  • Battery storage is the fastest-growing segment. "Battery energy storage system australia" leads at 1,000/mo — more than any solar term. Neither main competitor owns this space organically.
  • City-level terms behave differently by state. Melbourne and Brisbane lead at 260/mo each. Sydney is surprisingly lower at 140/mo — likely because NSW businesses search "commercial solar panels sydney" (50/mo) separately rather than the hub term.
  • Industrial hub searches are below detection floor (Wetherill Park, Dandenong South, Yatala, Wollongong — all 0/mo). These are strategic pages, not volume plays. The value is GBP/local pack signalling and long-tail conversions from buyers in those precincts.
  • Finance is an underserved gap. "Commercial solar finance" (50/mo, LOW competition, $12.15 CPC) has almost no competitor content. Easy ranking opportunity.

Keyword Strategy — Why This Structure

Strategy Rationale: 13 General + 15 Location

Why general pages are service pages (not city pages)

The original proposal mixed city pages into the general budget. This created a structural problem: city terms rank on location intent, while service terms rank on commercial intent. They require different page architectures and serve different funnel stages.

The revised structure separates these cleanly. General pages build national authority on what Energus does (installer, panels, BESS, industrial solar, finance). Location pages build geographic presence for where Energus operates. Each page does one job well.

13 service pages + 15 location pages = cleaner architecture
Why we dropped "industrial solar [city]" as general keywords

City-specific industrial solar terms (industrial solar sydney, industrial solar melbourne, etc.) all return zero measurable search volume in DataForSEO. They are not search terms buyers use — buyers search "commercial solar [city]" or "industrial solar" nationally.

Instead, one national "industrial solar" page (170/mo, $30.33 CPC) captures the entire industrial vertical. Industrial context is then added as secondary keyword on each city location page.

1 national industrial solar page replaces 5 zero-volume city variants
Why battery gets its own location pages

"Battery energy storage system australia" leads the entire plan at 1,000/mo — more than any solar keyword. BESS is the fastest-growing segment in C&I energy. The general BESS page (G4) covers national intent; city-level battery pages capture buyers who have narrowed their search to a specific state/city market.

We've added 5 battery location pages for the cities with measurable volume: Adelaide leads at 40/mo (driven by SA's high electricity prices and energy transition push), Brisbane at 20/mo, Sydney and Melbourne at 10/mo each, and Townsville at 10/mo.

Battery = separate service cluster, not just a secondary keyword
Why industrial hubs (Wetherill Park, Dandenong South, Yatala) with zero volume

These are Australia's top industrial precincts — where the factories, food processors, logistics hubs, and manufacturing plants are. Even with zero search volume for the suburb-level keyword, these pages serve three purposes: (1) GBP/local pack signalling — Google uses city pages to determine your service area; (2) long-tail conversions — a factory owner in Wetherill Park searching "commercial solar wetherill park" is a highly qualified lead even if it's one search per month; (3) client presentations — having a dedicated page for a prospect's suburb builds trust in discovery meetings.

Strategic pages — not volume plays, but high-value anyway

AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation

AEO Strategy for Energus

What AEO means for a B2B solar company

Answer Engine Optimisation targets AI-generated responses in Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT (GPT search), Perplexity, and Gemini. B2B procurement buyers increasingly use these tools for initial research — "how much does commercial solar cost", "what is the ROI on commercial solar", "how does a BESS work".

Being cited in AI answers builds brand awareness at the earliest stage of the buying journey — before the buyer has shortlisted any installer. For Energus, the goal is to become a trusted reference source for commercial solar and battery questions in Australia.

How to implement
  • Add a structured FAQ section to every service page (5–8 questions per page, direct answers)
  • Implement FAQ schema (JSON-LD) on all pages with FAQ sections — this feeds structured data directly to search engines and AI models
  • Write answer blocks in the format: question → 2–3 sentence direct answer → elaboration
  • Keep answers factual and specific to Australian C&I context (costs in AUD, references to SA/QLD electricity rates, CEC accreditation, etc.)
  • Publish at minimum one long-form AEO article targeting each major cost/ROI question
Priority AEO questions to target (by page)
How much does commercial solar cost in Australia?
Target page: /commercial-solar-quote/ or /commercial-solar-finance/ — include a cost breakdown table by system size (100kW, 250kW, 500kW+), payback period ranges, and mention of finance options.
What is the ROI on commercial solar?
Target page: /solar-for-business/ — reference Energus's average payback period, hipages stat ($3 return per $1 spent), and note that 55MW+ delivered by Energus.
How does a battery energy storage system work?
Target page: /battery-storage/ — explain charge/discharge cycle, grid interaction, demand charge reduction. Use "BESS" consistently as the technical term alongside the plain-English version.
What is the difference between commercial and industrial solar?
Target page: /industrial-solar/ — clarify the distinction (system size, structural requirements, industrial roof types, grid connection complexity).
How long does commercial solar installation take?
Target page: /commercial-solar-installer/ — give a realistic timeline (design → approval → install), mention Energus's in-house engineers and project managers.
Is commercial solar worth it for businesses in Australia?
Target page: /solar-for-business/ — CFO-level ROI case with specific numbers, electricity cost reduction figures, carbon reduction appeal for ESG reporting.

Q7: What information do I need to provide when engaging your repossession agents?
A: To begin a repossession matter, you need the asset details, the debtor's last known contact information and address, the outstanding debt or loan amount, and any relevant security agreement or loan documentation. Q7: What debtor details do I need to provide for skip tracing to be effective?
A: The more information you can provide, the more targeted our investigation will be. We typically need the debtor's full name and any last known details such as a prior address, phone number, employer name or vehicle registration. Even partial information is useful. Our investigators use every detail as a starting point and work outward through professional databases, field intelligence and verified inquiry channels to establish a current location.

Q8: How long does a skip tracing investigation typically take?
A: Timelines depend on how long the debtor has been uncontactable and the amount of background information available. Some debtors are located within a few days of us starting an investigation. More complex matters where the debtor has deliberately concealed their location may take longer. We keep you informed of progress throughout and move immediately into our debt recovery process once a debtor has been located.

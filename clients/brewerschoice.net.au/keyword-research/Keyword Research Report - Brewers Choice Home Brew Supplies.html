HAND-OFF AUDIT RESULTS — 3 changes vs initial pipeline output
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. NEAR-ME HELD OUT (GBP measurement track, not organic)
   "Near me" keywords are tracked separately as a GBP/local-pack
   measurement layer (reviews, GBP optimisation, maps SEO) rather
   than as content-page targets in the organic set. Per agency
   methodology — content pages cannot reliably convert "near me"
   intent; the conversion path is GBP card → call/visit, not page →
   form. Dropped from this set:
     home brew shop near me            2,400/mo  KD 7
     home brewing supplies near me     3,600/mo  KD 22
   Both will be tracked in the GBP-measurement layer instead of the
   organic-rankings layer. v0.4.2's near-me filter was technically
   permissive for hybrid_retail; we override per methodology.

2. LOCATION GRID CORRECTED (12 → 10, dual-silo)
   Phase 5 audit revealed the initial 12-cell grid was single-silo
   near-duplicate waste: every suburb had two Beer variants
   ("home brew shop {suburb}" + "home brewing supplies {suburb}") —
   same intent, same landing page, same silo — violating Phase 5
   SKILL Step 5.5 anti-pattern "max 1 keyword per topic cluster".
   The deterministic select_location_grid() returned only 6 cells
   (single-silo); silo_service_map={} was a script bug.

   Corrected to a dual-silo grid honoring the BDM-brief co-anchor
   priorities (Beer + Spirits both Tier A1/A2):

     Beer    — 4 suburbs (Ring 1 + Ring 2 strongholds only):
                Bayswater, Boronia, Croydon, Clayton
                Service: "home brew shop {suburb}"
                Target: /shop/

     Spirits — 6 suburbs (full client-named list):
                Bayswater, Boronia, Croydon, Clayton, Oakleigh,
                Springvale
                Service: "home distilling kit {suburb}"
                Target: /product-category/spirit-making/

   Why drop Beer at Oakleigh + Springvale (Ring 3)? Phase 2 flagged
   Oakleigh as australianhomebrewing.com.au's home turf (4.8★/770
   reviews dominating local pack) and Springvale as outside natural
   25km catchment. Trading those 2 weak Beer cells for full Spirits
   coverage gives the Spirits silo Ring 1-3 ring symmetry against a
   different competitive set (distilleryking.com.au is national, not
   Bayswater-local — clean carve-out).

3. R11 WINE FERMENTER REASONING STRENGTHENED
   Phase 3c probe (2026-05-11) found mixed-AU SERP for this term:
   AU at #1 only (qualityhomebrew.com.au), UK at #2 (themaltmiller),
   US at #3 (wineenthusiast). All 4 other audited bare keywords were
   .com.au-only top-3 (defensible-bare). R11 retained with reasoning
   strengthened to require AU-coded content (litre sizing, GST pricing,
   AU shipping signals) to defend the #1 position against international
   evergreen content.

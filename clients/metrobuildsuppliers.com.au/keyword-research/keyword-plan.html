Keyword Research Methodology

What We Did & What We Found

  • SE Ranking (AU source) — Keyword Difficulty scores across all candidates (more accurate for AU geo-modified terms than DataForSEO KD)
  • SERP composition analysis — manual verification of top-3 domain DAs and SERP type classification for every selected keyword
  • Competitor domain profiling — DA, referring domains, top-ranking keywords for all 5 named competitors + 2 organic discoveries
  • Brave Search + review mining — customer vocabulary, problem language, trade terminology extraction from Google Business Profile reviews

Key Research Findings
  • Brand + distributor queries are the winning lane. "Rockcote supplier melbourne" (KD 10), "Unitex render system melbourne" (KD 11), "NRG Greenboard supplier" (KD 8) all have very low KD because no Melbourne trade store has built dedicated distributor content. Official distributor status = first-mover advantage on every brand page.
  • Generic head terms are dominated by Bunnings and Bowens. "Building supplies melbourne" has Bunnings at #1 (DA 78) and Bowens at #2 (DA 52). These are 12+ month targets, not launch priorities.
  • Decorative finishes are the clearest quick wins. "Micro cement supplier melbourne" has a DA 12 site at #1. "Venetian plaster supplier melbourne" top 3 are all DA 14-18. Both achievable within 90 days of launch.
  • Location pages face zero specialist competition. All 10 suburb-level rendering queries return only directories (Yellow Pages, hipages, True Local). No competitor has dedicated suburb rendering supply pages.
  • CPC signals real buyer intent. James Hardie ($5.20), micro cement ($5.10), venetian plaster ($4.20), Hebel ($4.50) — all premium CPCs relative to their volumes, confirming high purchase intent from trade buyers.

Keyword Strategy

Strategy Rationale: 15G + 10L for a New-Domain Trade Supplier

New domain reality: avoid the generic trap

Metrobuild launched in October 2025 with near-zero DA. The temptation is to go after "building supplies melbourne" (390/mo) immediately — but Bunnings (DA 78) and Bowens (DA 52) own that SERP and won't be displaced for 12+ months regardless of content quality.

The strategy instead targets two lanes that are winnable NOW:

  • Brand + distributor pages — Rockcote, Unitex, Hebel, James Hardie, NRG. Official distributor status gives a content authority signal no competitor can fake. KD 8-19 across all brand pages.
  • Decorative/niche product pages — Micro cement (DA 12 at #1), venetian plaster (DA 14-18 in top 3). First-mover wins with quality content.
Win the winnable first — build DA — then compete for broader terms
Why "rendering supplies" rather than "building supplies" leads

Metrobuild's named competitors (Bell Plaster, Crowntex) play in the rendering lane at DA 18-28 — directly beatable. The building supplies lane is Bowens vs Bunnings — not beatable in year 1.

"Rendering supplies melbourne" (170/mo, KD 18) sits in a local_businesses SERP where Bell Plaster (DA 28) ranks #3. Metrobuild's Rockcote + Unitex official distributor status gives a stronger page than Bell Plaster can produce.

Specialist trade content beats general hardware stores in specialist SERPs
The distributor advantage — why brand pages rank

When a renderer searches "Rockcote supplier melbourne," they're purchase-ready. They've already chosen the brand — they need a local stockist. Rockcote's own site ranks #1 (expected). The position #2 slot goes to the most authoritative local distributor page — currently Crowntex at DA 18 with thin content.

A Metrobuild Rockcote distributor page with full product range, technical datasheets, and trade pricing will rank #2 within 3-6 months — and that pattern repeats for Unitex, Hebel, James Hardie, and NRG.

5 brand distributor pages = 5 quick wins in the 3-6 month window
Location page logic: 10 suburbs x 1 service silo

With 10 location pages and a budget constraint, the priority is geographic breadth over multiple silo coverage. "Rendering supplies" is selected as the single service silo because:

  • Highest trade search volume at suburb level (vs "building supplies {suburb}" where Bowens has pages)
  • Core audience (renderers/plasterers) is most likely to search by suburb
  • Official Rockcote + Unitex distributor status = unique content authority on each page

Suburbs selected to cover SE primary corridor (Dandenong, Glen Waverley, Clayton, Keysborough, Noble Park), outer SE growth zones (Cranbourne, Berwick), western expansion (Truganina), northern expansion (Craigieburn), and inner commercial (Richmond).

10 suburb pages cover all four geographic delivery zones from Springvale

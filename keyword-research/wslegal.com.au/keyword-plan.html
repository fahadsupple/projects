Entity Structure Being Built
This keyword plan is not just a list of search terms — it is a structured entity architecture that teaches Google what this business is, where it operates, and what it specialises in. Each tier of the site builds a distinct layer of that identity.
  • Tier 1 — Homepage (Brand + Primary Service + Location): The homepage entity signals: "Wollerman Shacklock Lawyers is a Melbourne property law firm, led by Trish Sheedy, an Accredited Property Law Specialist." The primary keyword ("property lawyer melbourne") establishes the firm's core identity. The secondary ("property law specialist melbourne") anchors the credential differentiator — one of only ~75 LIV Accredited Property Specialists in Victoria. The homepage is also the Clyde North local entity via NAP, address schema, and content references.
  • Tier 2 — Service Pages (Topic Entities): Each service page builds a distinct topic entity that expands Google's understanding of what this firm does. Wills & Estate Planning, Probate & Deceased Estates, Business Law, and Commercial Lease Law are each independent topic clusters connected back to the homepage entity. Together they tell Google: "This is not a generalist firm — this firm is authoritative in these specific legal areas."
  • Tier 3 — Location Pages (Geographic Entities): The 20 location pages (10 suburbs × 2 roots) build geographic entity associations within the City of Casey. The "conveyancer [suburb]" pages establish that this firm handles property transactions in each suburb. The "property lawyer [suburb]" pages reinforce that the firm provides legal advice — not just document processing — in each location. Across all 10 suburbs, these pages collectively tell Google: "This firm is the Casey-area property law specialist."
  • The overall picture Google builds: Homepage (property law identity) → Service pages (topic authority) → Location pages (geographic authority in Casey). The full entity structure positions wslegal as the authoritative property and conveyancing legal practice for the City of Casey.
Entity architecture: brand identity (homepage) → topic authority (service pages) → geographic authority (location pages)
Why 10G + 20L (BDM Split)
The plan uses the BDM's recommended split of 10G + 20L. A conveyancer Melbourne service page was evaluated and removed for two reasons:
  • Conveyancing covered by location pages: Ten Casey suburb pages targeting "conveyancer [suburb]" already provide comprehensive conveyancing coverage across the client's actual service area. A separate Melbourne-level conveyancer page would compete against established Melbourne-wide specialist conveyancers — a difficult ranking environment for a firm starting from zero commercial rankings.
  • Local pages convert better for conveyancing: Property buyers and sellers search for conveyancers near the property or their own suburb. A Casey-based firm is better positioned to capture "conveyancer berwick" than "conveyancer melbourne." The 4 extra location pages (2 new suburbs × 2 roots) generate more leads faster than a competitive Melbourne service page.
Decision: 10G + 20L — BDM split, faster commercial lead generation via location pages
Why 2 Root Keywords (Conveyancer + Property Lawyer)
Unlike most trade contractor SEO projects where a single high-volume root dominates suburb searches, wslegal requires two roots for different strategic reasons:
  • Root 1 — "conveyancer [suburb]": Volume-driven. GKP confirms 170/mo for Berwick, Cranbourne, Pakenham, and Narre Warren. "Conveyancer" outperforms "conveyancing" at suburb level (Narre Warren: 170 vs 110 — a 55% volume gain). These pages capture people actively buying or selling property in each suburb — the most direct lead type for Trish's practice.
  • Root 2 — "property lawyer [suburb]": Brand-driven. Trish is an Accredited Property Law Specialist — one of only ~75 in all of Victoria. A conveyancer is not a lawyer. Suburb pages targeting "property lawyer [suburb]" differentiate wslegal from the licensed conveyancers who dominate the conveyancing SERP, and capture searchers who specifically want legal advice rather than document processing. Each "property lawyer" searcher represents a higher-value enquiry. This also directly fulfils the BDM brief which called out "Property Lawyers Melbourne" or "Property Lawyers Clyde" as target terms.
  • Why this pairing works: In the Austral Contractors project, a second root (core drilling) was rejected because suburb-level GKP volume was zero. Here, the second root is justified not by volume but by positioning and lead quality — one property law client engaged for complex advice is worth more than several basic conveyancing transactions.
Decision: 2 roots — conveyancer for volume, property lawyer for positioning

Beyond This Keyword Plan — What Else Is Needed
The keyword plan defines which pages to create and what to optimise them for. Several additional elements are required outside the scope of this plan to make the entity structure work and drive local rankings:
  • Google Business Profile (GBP) optimisation: Critical for local pack rankings, especially "near me" and suburb-level searches. GBP should list all service categories, set service area as City of Casey, include Trish's accreditation in the description, and have regular posts and review responses. "Conveyancer near me" and "property lawyer near me" searches are won through GBP, not service pages.
  • Schema markup: LegalService schema (type: Attorney) on homepage and all service pages. Include name, address, phone, areaServed (City of Casey), hasOfferCatalog (all services), employee (Trish Sheedy + LIV Accredited Specialist credential). FAQ schema on pages where common questions are answered (wills, probate, conveyancing). LocalBusiness schema on location pages with suburb-specific areaServed.
  • NAP consistency: Name, Address, Phone must match exactly across the website, Google Business Profile, and all legal/business directories (LIV member directory, True Local, Yellow Pages, Yelp, local Casey directories). Inconsistent NAP weakens local entity signals.
  • Internal linking structure: Homepage → all service pages. Service pages → relevant location pages (e.g., Probate Melbourne → link to Berwick and Narre Warren pages). Location pages → link back to the matching service page. Blog posts (especially the quick wins) → link to the most relevant service page. This passes entity signals across the site hierarchy.
  • Content production: Each of the 25 pages (5 service + 20 location) needs original content built around the supporting entities listed above. Location pages especially should not be template swaps — suburb-specific property market context, local references, and genuine differentiation improve both rankings and conversion. Thin or duplicated content across location pages will undermine the keyword plan.
  • Citation building: LIV member directory listing, Law Society directories, and local business citations (True Local, Yellow Pages). Citations are a significant local ranking factor and reinforce the business entity's legitimacy and location signals.
  • Review strategy: Google reviews directly impact local pack rankings and click-through rates. A plan to generate reviews from satisfied clients — particularly for conveyancing completions — will accelerate local visibility. wslegal's personal, values-driven approach is a natural asset here.
  • Blog quick wins (separate to this plan): wslegal has three blog posts on page 2 (pos 11–30) for high-volume, low-KD informational terms: "tenants in common" (2,500/mo, KD:1), "intestate" (900/mo, KD:8), "medical power of attorney" (450/mo, KD:11). Optimising these three posts is a fast, no-new-page win that can generate organic traffic within weeks. These should be treated as a priority alongside the page build-out.
Note: The keyword plan alone will not drive leads. GBP, schema, NAP, internal links, and quality content are all required for the entity structure to perform.

Why 1 Root Keyword (Concrete Cutting) Instead of 2
The original plan split location pages across two root keywords: concrete cutting (12 suburbs) and core drilling (12 suburbs). After analysis, we consolidated to 1 root with 16 suburbs. Reasons:
  • Search volume: "Concrete cutting [suburb]" has confirmed GKP volume in 15 of the 16 chosen suburbs (10–20/mo each). "Core drilling [suburb]" returned zero volume across all 12 suburbs tested — no one searches for core drilling by suburb name.
  • Lead generation: 16 pages with real search volume = 16 direct lead opportunities. 8 zero-volume core drilling suburb pages would generate no organic traffic. The trade-off — 8 more concrete cutting suburb pages vs 8 core drilling suburb pages — clearly favours concrete cutting.
  • Core drilling is not left uncovered: The dedicated service page /concrete-core-drilling/ targets "core drilling melbourne" (30/mo, $22.04 bid — highest in the dataset) and captures "core drilling near me" (140/mo, $18.34 bid) via Google Business Profile local pack. This is where the actual core drilling demand sits — at city level, not suburb level.
  • Client ROI: A client who doesn't see traffic or leads from pages loses confidence. 16 suburb pages that rank and get clicked give visible results. 8 zero-volume suburb pages do not.
Decision: 1 root × 16 suburbs maximises lead generation within budget
Why These 6 Service Pages
Service pages target city-level intent (Melbourne) for specific services. Each was selected based on SERP validation — we confirmed the search results for each term are dominated by concrete cutting specialists (Austral's direct competitors), not unrelated businesses.
  • Homepage: "Concrete cutting melbourne" (390/mo) — competitors' homepages dominate this SERP. Correct page type.
  • Ring + wall sawing: Specialist sawing methods. Thin competition, almost no dedicated pages exist from competitors = easier ranking. Ring sawing generic term = 880/mo at $103 bid, page captures local intent.
  • Core drilling: Highest bid in dataset ($22–$27). B2B buyers (builders, civil contractors). Competitors use dedicated inner pages, not homepages, for this term.
  • Concrete removal: 140/mo, LOW competition. Broad competitive set (demolition firms, excavation companies) = differentiation opportunity for Austral's specialist robotic demolition approach.
  • Wire sawing: Used for marine, rail, bridge, and defence projects — Austral's highest-value sectors. SERP is 100% concrete specialists. Client explicitly requested this service.
  • Road sawing: Highest volume of all newly added terms (concrete road sawing = 50/mo). SERP is 100% concrete specialists. Every major competitor (MEGASAW, Core and Saw, VicSawing) has a dedicated page for this.
All 6 service SERPs confirmed as concrete cutting specialist territory
Suburb Selection Criteria
Suburbs were selected from a 491-keyword GKP export run specifically for Melbourne suburb variants. Primary criterion was confirmed GKP search volume. Secondary criteria: geographic spread across Melbourne and client's stated priority locations.
  • Primary criterion — B2B audience fit: Austral targets commercial builders, civil contractors, and industrial facility managers. Suburbs were selected based on commercial/industrial construction activity, not just search volume. Residential suburbs generate the wrong leads for a B2B specialist.
  • 12 suburbs with GKP volume retained: Dandenong, Cranbourne, Berwick, Frankston, Richmond, Epping, Craigieburn, Werribee, Pakenham, Preston, Blackburn, plus Bendigo (client-requested).
  • 4 zero-volume suburbs added on commercial grounds: Clayton (Monash industrial precinct — cleanest specialist SERP, no competitor suburb pages = ranking gap), Somerton (Somerton Business Park, purest SERP of all candidates tested, no suburb pages from competitors), Moorabbin (established south industrial precinct, Wylies 40-year presence confirmed), Bayswater (Knox/Maroondah east industrial belt, specialist pages exist).
  • Replaced — Brighton, Ascot Vale, Croydon, Lilydale: All predominantly residential suburbs. Brighton (affluent bayside), Ascot Vale (inner-west residential), Croydon and Lilydale (outer-east residential/rural fringe). Searches from these suburbs would generate residential renovation enquiries — wrong audience for Austral.
  • Considered but not included — Springvale, Campbellfield: Both industrial but superseded by Clayton and Somerton respectively after SERP comparison. Mulgrave: Good SERP but geographically redundant with Clayton (same Monash Freeway corridor, SE already has 5 suburbs). Truganina: Major western industrial growth corridor but SERP mixed with general concreters — cleaner alternatives prioritised.
  • SERP validated: All 4 replacement suburbs confirmed — concrete cutting specialists dominate results, not general concreters or unrelated businesses.
  • Geographic spread achieved: South East (Dandenong, Springvale, Cranbourne, Berwick, Pakenham), South Coast (Frankston), South (Moorabbin), Inner (Richmond), East (Bayswater, Blackburn), Outer North (Epping, Campbellfield, Craigieburn), North (Preston), West (Werribee), Regional (Bendigo).
12/16 suburbs have GKP volume · all 16 SERP-validated · B2B audience alignment prioritised over raw search volume
Keywords Evaluated and Excluded
The following client-requested or candidate keywords were researched and excluded with reasoning:
  • Hand sawing: Generic term (4,400/mo) is dominated by DIY woodworking product searches. Melbourne SERP with concrete context shows specialists — but "hand sawing melbourne" and "concrete hand sawing melbourne" both returned zero GKP volume. No valid keyword pair available for a dedicated page. Covered in body copy on existing sawing page.
  • Scabbling: 210/mo but $0 advertiser bid — no commercial buyers running ads for this term. Likely informational searches by engineers and researchers, not procurement managers calling contractors. Covered in body copy on scarifying/shaving page.
  • Concrete grinding: 5,400/mo but SERP is entirely floor polishing and residential renovation companies — a different business vertical. Ranking for this would generate the wrong leads (floor polishing enquiries, not cutting/demolition contracts).
  • Concrete disposal / concrete dump: Different searcher intent entirely — people with existing rubble looking for skip bins or tips. SERP dominated by waste management companies. Low bids ($1–$4) confirm low commercial value for a specialist contractor.
  • Bare root keywords (concrete cutting, core drilling, wire sawing, road sawing): Excluded per strategy rule — all keywords must carry a location or service modifier. Bare roots attract broad national traffic that does not convert for a local service business.
All exclusions based on SERP intent mismatch or absence of a valid modifier with GKP volume
SERP Audit Results — All Selected Keywords Checked
All 12 service/general keywords and a sample of location keywords were checked against live search results. Results were evaluated for competitive set alignment (concrete specialists vs unrelated businesses).
  • Clean (concrete specialists dominate): concrete cutting melbourne, ring sawing melbourne, wall sawing melbourne, core drilling melbourne, concrete coring melbourne, road sawing melbourne, wire sawing melbourne, concrete cutting dandenong, concrete cutting frankston, concrete cutting werribee.
  • Road sawing page — primary/secondary swapped: "concrete road sawing" (50/mo) without location modifier returns informational content and US companies (Wikipedia, Husqvarna, US federal highway guides). Swapped to make "road sawing melbourne" the primary — SERP is 100% concrete specialists. "Concrete road sawing" retained as secondary for volume capture.
  • Concrete removal / demolition — broad competitive set confirmed: SERP includes rubbish removal companies, earthworks firms, and general demolition contractors alongside specialist concrete cutters. Page is valid but content must differentiate Austral's robotic/precision demolition approach from residential driveway removal services.
  • Concrete cutting craigieburn — mixed but low competition: General concreters (slab layers, driveway companies) appear alongside cutting specialists. No dominant cutting specialist owning this SERP — easier ranking opportunity for Austral.
  • Generic terms (concrete cutting services, wire sawing services): Search tool is US-based so these returned US companies. In an Australian Google session these terms show Australian concrete specialists. No change required.
